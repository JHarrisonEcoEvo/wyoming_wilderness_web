In 2024 I hiked across the Gros Ventre mountain range with a good friend. Both of us were not expecting that the star of the show would be the geology–we are not particularly geological nerds. But the ground is arresting throughout this hike.

Somewhere on the east side of Gros Ventre peak we found a patch of geodes. They were the size, shape, and coloration of burritos. As we dropped into the xx creek drainage, we encountered bare limestone, cut with crevasses. Like a glacier, one can hop over a foot wide crack and not see the bottom. We found a larger crevasse with the bones of an elk in its bowels.

## Safety

When hiking the high country in this range, water is in really scarce supply. There are not as many lakes as in some other Wyoming ranges. A friend and I hiked across Gros Ventre peak and the xx plateau. We started with 9 liters of water and ran out after 18 miles or so. It was a dry hike until we made it to xx creek.

If you hike early season you could find some snow drifts perhaps.

Lightning risk would be high here. You will be the tallest thing on the plateau.

Grizzly bears are in the area.
